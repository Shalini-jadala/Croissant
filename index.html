A croissant is a buttery, flaky pastry made using laminated dough. It is famous for its crisp outer layers and soft, airy inside. Originally inspired by Austrian baking traditions, croissants are now a symbol of French cuisine.

Why Croissants Are Popular

Croissants are loved worldwide because of their rich butter flavor, light texture, and versatility. They can be enjoyed plain or filled with chocolate, almond cream, cheese, or ham.

Types of Croissants

  • Butter Croissant
  • Chocolate Croissant (Pain au Chocolat)
  • Almond Croissant
  • Cheese Croissant
